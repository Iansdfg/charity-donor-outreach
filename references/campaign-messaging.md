# Campaign Messaging

Use supplied, approved facts only. Campaign type controls tone, never factual authorization.

## Human tone

Every letter should sound like one caring person writing to another:

1. **Warm greeting:** follow the safe salutation with a natural opening rather than moving immediately to money.
2. **Grounded gratitude:** thank the donor for verified support. If no reliable giving history exists, thank them for their connection or attention without inventing prior support.
3. **Caring connection:** describe the approved shared work in plain, compassionate language and recognize the donor as part of the community without asserting how they feel or why they give.
4. **Respectful invitation:** use “we invite you to consider” or similarly low-pressure wording. Never imply obligation, guilt, disappointment, or that care depends on giving.
5. **Appreciative close:** add a separate closing paragraph thanking the reader for their time, care, or consideration whether or not they make a gift.

Prefer conversational sentences, contractions where natural, and varied wording. Avoid corporate jargon, excessive superlatives, repeated “thank you,” false intimacy, emotional manipulation, and claims such as “we know how deeply you care” unless explicitly supplied and approved.

The following terse legacy patterns are not acceptable as complete paragraphs:

- “Our records show lifetime giving of …”
- “Your support sustains our community programs.”
- “Would you consider a gift of …?”

The underlying facts may be used, but weave them into natural gratitude, community context, a respectful invitation, and a separate appreciative close.

| Campaign | Safe tone and focus | Claims needing explicit supplied approval |
|---|---|---|
| Emergency Appeal | Direct, calm, timely; explain the stated need | urgency, deadline, match, impact/outcome figures |
| Annual Fund | Warm, community-oriented; consistency and broad mission | giving streak, program statistics, legacy giving |
| Capital Campaign | Formal, future-focused; approved project purpose | naming opportunities, construction milestones, costs |
| Event Fundraiser | Friendly and welcoming; supplied event purpose | registration counts, attendance, premiums, deadlines |
| Unknown | Neutral gratitude and invitation | every campaign-specific fact |

## Claim controls

- Matching language is allowed only when campaign data says the match is **confirmed** and supplies the approved wording. “Pending,” “unconfirmed,” silence, or donor-cell text means no match language.
- Naming opportunities, legacy giving, tote bags/premiums, event counts, tax statements, deadlines, impact statistics, and relationship-manager identity require explicit approved input.
- Never imply an offer through suggestive wording when the fact is not approved.
- Never convert campaign type into invented urgency. An Emergency Appeal alone does not authorize “every hour counts.”

## Safe fallbacks

- Gratitude without reliable giving history: “Thank you for taking the time to learn about this work.”
- Annual/unknown: “Your support helps sustain the work we share as a community.”
- Emergency: “We are inviting supporters to help with this response.” Use only if the campaign itself is confirmed as an emergency response.
- Capital: “We invite you to support this approved campaign priority.”
- Event: “We invite you to support this community event.”
- Closing appreciation: “Thank you for your time, your consideration, and all the ways you help strengthen our community.”

Omit a sentence rather than fill it with an unsupported fact.
